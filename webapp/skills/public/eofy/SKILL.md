# End of Financial Year (EOFY) Guide

## Key Date: 30 June

The Australian financial year runs from 1 July to 30 June.

## Critical EOFY Tasks

### STP Finalisation
- **Due by 14 July** (or 31 July if using a tax agent)
- Finalise all employee payment summaries through Single Touch Payroll
- Mark as "final" in your payroll software
- Employees can then access their income statement via myGov

### Superannuation
- **Q4 super due 28 July**
- Ensure all super guarantee payments are made before 30 June for a tax deduction
- Current SG rate: check ATO for current financial year rate
- Review any unpaid super from prior quarters

### Stocktake
- Complete physical stocktake as close to 30 June as possible
- Value stock at lower of cost or net realisable value
- Write off obsolete or damaged stock

### Asset Depreciation
- Calculate depreciation for all fixed assets
- Review instant asset write-off eligibility (check current threshold)
- Assess assets for impairment
- Update fixed asset register

### Bad Debts
- Review outstanding debtors
- Write off genuinely bad debts before 30 June
- Must be able to demonstrate debt is bad, not merely doubtful

### Trust Distributions
- **Trust resolutions must be made by 30 June**
- Ensure trustee resolution for distribution of income
- Document all distribution decisions

### Division 7A
- Review all shareholder/director loans
- Ensure minimum repayments are made before 30 June
- Put in place complying loan agreements if required

### Tax Planning
- Prepay expenses (up to 12 months in advance for SBEs)
- Defer income where appropriate and legal
- Review capital gains and losses
- Consider timing of asset purchases

## Post-30 June
- Lodge Q4 BAS (due 28 July, or later with tax agent)
- Lodge income tax return
- Review and finalise all year-end adjustments
- Start planning for the new financial year
